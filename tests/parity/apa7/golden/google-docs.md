The foundational treatment of pragmatic markers in contact situations came from Smith (2010), whose framework has shaped subsequent work. Recent quantitative evidence reinforced the general claim (Smith, 2010, p. 22). Classroom-discourse data produced complementary findings (Jones & Cruz, 2015), while the urban multilingual synthesis broadened the empirical base (Able et al., 2019).

A consolidated view across the three main lines of evidence is now available (Able et al., 2019; Jones & Cruz, 2015; Smith, 2010).
