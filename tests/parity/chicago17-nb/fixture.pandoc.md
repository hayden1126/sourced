The argument developed by @smith-2010 ran counter to earlier claims about
pragmatic markers in contact situations. Smith's later treatment [@smith-2010]
extended the point with closer reading of the data. The parallel case in
classroom discourse received fresh attention from @jones-cruz-2015, who showed
similar dynamics at scale [@jones-cruz-2015, p. 88]. Broader empirical work
covered urban multilingual contexts as well [@able-baker-chen-2019].

A synthesis across all three lines of evidence is now available [@smith-2010;
@jones-cruz-2015; @able-baker-chen-2019].

Institutional reporting on the same period reinforces the point [@mla-2021]. An
undated commentary in the trade press touched on adjacent issues [@perennial-nd].
