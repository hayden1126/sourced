# Fixture: ieee parity

Recent work on pragmatic markers in contact situations established the
baseline for cross-linguistic studies \[1\]. Code-switching dynamics
in classroom discourse received a complementary treatment \[2\],
and a broader empirical synthesis covered urban multilingual contexts
\[3\]. The first study went on to address quantitative
predictions \[1, p. 22\], building on the original framework.

Methodological consolidation across the three lines of evidence is now
available \[1\], \[2\], \[3\].

Institutional reporting on similar trends adds context \[4\].
